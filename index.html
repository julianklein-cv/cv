Curriculum Vitae

I am a PhD student at the software and embedded systems engineering (SESE) group of the institute of software engineering and theoretical computer science at TU Berlin. The focus of my research is to guarantee confidentiality properties in time-sensitive systems. Besides my work as a researcher, I am also a teaching assistant. This includes supervision of theses, project work with small groups of students, and a large undergraduate courses with over 700 students.

Education

I completed my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in computer science at TU Berlin. Throughout my studies, I found topics in theoretical computer science and especially regarding formal verification most interesting. In both my theses, I worked on formal verification methods of confidentiality properties of timed systems. In particular, as part of my master thesis, I first proved the opacity verification problem for discrete-timed automata to be decidable - a result that has been independently re-established multiple times since. I continue the topic of verification and enforcement of opacity of discrete-timed automata in my research as a PhD student. My goal with my PhD is to develop scalable methods to guarantee timed opacity that can be used on actual real-world applications (see publications for more information).
